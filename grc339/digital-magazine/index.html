Mt. Rainier Skyline Trail

Hiking the Mt. Rainier Skyline Trail offers a challenging yet rewarding experience with its steep ascents and diverse terrain, providing spectacular views of Mt. Rainier, alpine meadows, and glacier-capped ridges. This 14-mile trek showcases the region's stunning landscapes and rich wildlife, making it a must-do adventure for seasoned hikers.

Rattlesnake Ledge

Hiking Rattlesnake Ledge offers a moderately challenging trail that rewards hikers with stunning views of Rattlesnake Lake and the surrounding mountains. The trail is popular for its scenic beauty, especially at sunrise or sunset, making it a favorite among both locals and visitors.

Story about Tolmie Peak Trail

Hiking Tolmie Peak provides a moderately strenuous trek through dense forests and along beautiful alpine meadows, culminating in breathtaking panoramic views of Mount Rainier and Eunice Lake from the Tolmie Peak Fire Lookout. The trail is a popular choice for its scenic diversity and stunning vantage points, especially on clear days.

Snoqualmie Falls

Snoqualmie Falls is a spectacular 268-foot waterfall, easily accessible to visitors year-round, known for its powerful cascades and misty spray. The falls are surrounded by lush forested trails and viewing platforms, making it a popular destination for both sightseeing and photography.

Poo Poo Point

Poo Poo Point is a popular hiking destination that offers a moderately challenging ascent with rewarding views of the Issaquah Valley and Lake Sammamish. The summit is also a favored launch site for paragliders, providing a scenic vantage point for both hikers and spectators.

Suncadia

Suncadia Trails offer a network of scenic pathways through the picturesque Suncadia Resort area, featuring a variety of trails suitable for hiking, biking, and wildlife viewing. The trails meander through diverse landscapes, including forests, meadows, and along the Cle Elum River, providing opportunities for outdoor recreation and relaxation in a serene setting.
